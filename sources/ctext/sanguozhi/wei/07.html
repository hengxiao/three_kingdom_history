《呂布傳 - Biography of Lü Bu 》

 English translation: AI and Chinese Text Project users [?] Library Resources

呂布傳:
呂布字奉先，五原郡九原人也。以驍武給并州。刺史丁原為騎都尉，屯河內，以布為主簿，大見親待。靈帝崩，原將兵詣洛陽。
Lü Bu, courtesy name Fengxian, was a native of Jiuyuan, Wuyuan Jun. He was assigned to Bingzhou as a valiant warrior. The prefect Ding Yuan served as Qidouwei, stationed in Hedong, and appointed Lü Bu as his zhoubu. Lü Bu was greatly favored and treated with intimacy. After Emperor Ling died, Yuan led troops to Luoyang.

呂布傳:
英雄記曰：原子建陽。本出自寒家，為人麤略，有武勇，善騎射。為南縣吏，受使不辭難，有警急，追寇虜，輙在其前。裁知書，少有吏用。與何進謀誅諸黃門，拜執金吾。進敗，董卓入京都，將為亂，欲殺原，并其兵衆。卓以布見信於原，誘布令殺原。布斬原首詣卓，卓以布為騎都尉，甚愛信之，誓為父子。
The Records of Heroes and Warriors states: Yuan's courtesy name was Jianyang. He originally came from a humble family, had a rough and unrefined character, but possessed martial valor and was skilled in horseback riding and archery. He served as a local official in Nan Xian, accepted missions without shying away from difficulties, and whenever there was an emergency or alarm, he would always lead the charge against bandits. He had only a basic knowledge of writing and showed administrative ability at an early age. He conspired with He Jin to eliminate the eunuchs, and was appointed Zhijinwu. After He Jin's defeat, Dong Zhuo entered the capital, intending to create chaos. He planned to kill Yuan and take over his troops. Dong Zhuo, knowing that Lü Bu was trusted by Yuan, lured Lü Bu into killing Yuan. Lü Bu beheaded Yuan and presented the head to Dong Zhuo, who then appointed Lü Bu as Qidouwei. Dong Zhuo greatly loved and trusted him, vowing a father-son bond between them.

呂布傳:
布便弓馬，膂力過人，號為飛將。稍遷至中郎將，封都亭侯。卓自以遇人無禮，恐人謀己，行止常以布自衞。然卓性剛而褊，忿不思難，嘗小失意，拔手戟擲布。布拳捷避之，詩曰：「無拳無勇，職為亂階。」注：「拳，力也。」為卓顧謝，卓意亦解。由是陰怨卓。卓常使布守中閤，布與卓侍婢私通，恐事發覺，心不自安。
Lü Bu was skilled in archery and horsemanship, possessed extraordinary physical strength, and was known as the "flying general." He gradually advanced to Zhonglangjiang and was granted the title of Duting Hou. Dong Zhuo considered himself rude in dealing with others, feared that people might plot against him, and therefore always had Lü Bu accompany him for protection. However, Dong Zhuo was by nature rigid and narrow-minded; when angry, he did not consider the consequences. On one occasion, after a minor disappointment, he drew a halberd and threw it at Lü Bu. Lü Bu quickly dodged the attack. A verse says: "Without fists or courage, one becomes a source of disorder." Note: "Quan means strength." Lü Bu turned around and apologized to Dong Zhuo; thus, Dong Zhuo's anger was calmed. From then on, Lü Bu secretly resented Dong Zhuo. Dong Zhuo often assigned Lü Bu to guard the central gate, but Lü Bu had an affair with one of Dong Zhuo's concubines. Fearing that this secret might be discovered, he became increasingly uneasy in his heart.

How could I have foreseen that the Son of Heaven would not be pleased, leading to our native commandery being invaded? The Tai Shou encountered a calamity in Youli, and Chenliu fell victim to a military plot. Our plans were delayed, resulting in the loss of loyalty and filial virtue; holding my staff while turning my back on you has betrayed the bond between friends. Considering these two matters, I find that between being forced to act and losing the name of loyalty and filial piety or betraying the way of friendship, their weight differs greatly, as do their closeness. Therefore, I have wiped away my tears and severed our ties. If only my lord had shown a little more consideration for an old friend, treating those who remain with respect and those who leave with self-restraint, not hastily severing friendships but instead relying on discipline and punishment to assist himself, then I would have upheld the aspirations of Ji Zha and never resorted to battle today. What is an example of this? In the past, Zhang Jingming personally ascended the altar and swore an oath of blood, carrying out orders with haste. In the end, he convinced Han Mu to surrender his seal, allowing my lord to gain territory; Yet afterward, merely because he submitted a memorial to the Son of Heaven, was granted a title and had his name recorded in official histories, within a short time he did not receive forgiveness for past mistakes but instead suffered total annihilation. Min Songzhi's Annotation: The Yingxiong Ji states, "Yuan Shao sent Zhang Jingming, Guo Gongze, Gao Yuancan and others to persuade Han Fu to cede Ji Zhou." Nevertheless, the fact that Fu relinquished his position was also due in part to Jingming's efforts. The details of other matters are not known. Lü Fengxian came fleeing after attacking Dong Zhuo, requesting troops but was denied; what crime did he commit by leaving? He was again subjected to attacks and nearly died. Liu Zihuang, on a diplomatic mission, exceeded the time limit and was denied permission to leave. Fearing your authority but also thinking of his family, he resorted to deception in order to return home. He can be said to have demonstrated determination toward loyalty and filial devotion without damaging the principles of hegemony; Yet he was summarily executed under your command, receiving no leniency or pardon. Min Songzhi's Annotation: Gongsun Zan listed Yuan Shao's crimes in a memorial, stating, "Yuan Shao and the former Huya Jiangjun Liu Xun first jointly raised troops. Although Xun continued to be effective, he was wrongfully killed by Shao out of petty resentment; this is Yuan Shao's seventh crime." I suspect that this refers to Zihuang. Although I am not particularly wise and have never been able to discern the beginning from the end or recognize subtle signs of great events, I secretly believe that my lord's heart does not consider these three men deserving death, nor do their punishments fit the crime? In fact, you wish to unify the entire Shandong region and increase your forces to punish enemies. You fear that soldiers may hesitate or doubt, so you have no choice but to suppress imperial authority in order to elevate those who follow your decrees. Those who seek righteousness are honored, while those awaiting release are executed—this is for your benefit, not the wish of wandering officials like me. Therefore, I have learned from past examples and now fight to the death in adversity. Although I am a man of low intelligence, I too have heard the words of gentlemen. This is truly not in my heart. It was you, the master, who invited me. The reason I have abandoned my country and people to devote myself to this city is precisely because a gentleman's defiance does not serve the enemy state. Therefore, I have incurred your anger and been attacked for a long time. Yet you now cite this principle as advice to me—this seems like words that appear similar but lead in different directions; is it not inconsistent with what you would do if we shared joy or sorrow?

I have heard it said that righteousness does not betray one's family, and loyalty does not defy the sovereign. Therefore, by returning to my native commandery for support from relatives and aiding the Tai Shou in order to stabilize the state, I achieved both filial duty and loyalty with a single action—how can this be wrong? Yet you wish for me to abandon my roots and destroy my family, treating your lord and mine as equal. My lord is older than I by years, and we share a bond of deep friendship. If our principles diverge and I must part ways to ensure the safety of my sovereign and family, this can be called proper conduct. If you mean what you say, then Bao Xu should have died at the hands of Wu Yuan instead of weeping and crying in the court of Qin. To be overly concerned with avoiding trouble is to ignore words that contradict reason and principle. Perhaps you see that the city's siege remains unresolved and relief forces have not yet arrived, so moved by our marital bond and lifelong friendship, you believe it better to surrender and live in disgrace than to uphold righteousness and face ruin. In the past, Yan Ying did not lower his will before a drawn sword, and Nan Shi did not bend his brush to seek life. Thus their figures were recorded in paintings, and their names endured through posterity. Moreover, I now hold a city as solid as gold, command the strength of soldiers and civilians, have spent three years' reserves to provide for one year's needs, and strive to relieve hardship and supplement shortages so as to please all under heaven—how could I ever expect to build a house only to return to farming! But I fear that when autumn winds raise dust, Bo Gui will turn his horse southward, Zhang Yang and Feiyan will use their strength to create trouble, the northern frontier will soon report a dire emergency of hanging upside down, and my trusted ministers will submit sincere petitions for me to return. In the past, Emperor Gaozu recruited Peng Yue from Ju Ye, and Emperor Guangwu laid the foundation of his reign at Lulin. In the end they were able to rise like dragons in a new era and accomplish imperial achievements. If one can assist the sovereign in promoting civilization, what shame is there? Moreover, I personally received the imperial decree and have worked with them.

The city's grain supplies had been exhausted, and there were no strong reinforcements from outside. Zang Hong realized he could not avoid his fate and called to the officials and soldiers, saying: "The Yuan family is tyrannical and has plotted treason; furthermore, they did not come to save my commandery." The officials, soldiers, and civilians all wept and said: "You, Your Honor, have no grudge with the Yuan family. Now you have brought hardship upon yourself for the sake of your native commandery's Tai Shou. How could we, your subjects and people, bear to leave you?" At first they dug up rats and boiled tendons and horn, but later there was nothing left to eat. The chief clerk offered three dou of rice from the inner kitchen and requested that it be divided to make porridge. Zang Hong sighed and said, "What is the point of eating this alone!" When the city fell, Cao Shao captured Zang Hong alive. Cao Shao had always been fond of Zang Hong and arranged a grand reception with canopies, summoning all his generals to meet him. He said: "Zang Hong, how could you betray me in this way!" Have you finally submitted today?" Zang Hong glared at him and said: "The Y uans have served the Han dynasty for four generations, producing five high officials. One could say they were favored by grace. Now that the royal house is weak, you have no intention of supporting it but instead seek to take advantage of the situation and hope for unexpected gains. You kill many loyal men in order to establish a tyrannical authority. I personally saw you address Zhang Chenliu as your elder brother, so my late father should also be considered your younger brother. Together we should work with all our strength to eliminate the nation's enemies—why then do you gather an army and watch others being slaughtered! I regret that my power is insufficient, otherwise I would have drawn a blade to avenge the wrongs of the world. How can you say I have submitted!" Cao Shao originally loved Zang Hong and had intended for him to submit, so he spared him; But upon hearing the intensity of Zang Hong's words, Cao Shao realized that he would never be useful to himself and so had him killed. Xu Zhong, in his Three Kingdoms Commentary, said: Zang Hong upheld the principles of righteousness and loyalty throughout the world. He rescued his former sovereign from danger; his kindness was sufficient to move people's hearts, and his sense of justice could inspire a shallow society. Yet the Yuan family also regarded him as their own kinsman and friend, appointing him to positions in commanderies and states. Though not sovereign and subject, they were indeed allied lords; having accepted their appointment, by righteousness he should not have been disloyal. At that time, Yuan Shao and Cao Cao were still on good terms, jointly supporting the royal house. But Lu Bu was fickle and without righteousness, with ambitions of rebellion; Liu Mo and Cheng Chao arbitrarily appointed him as Tai Shou of a state. In regard to royal law, they are nothing but criminals. When Cao Cao attacked them, the Yuan family did not come to their rescue; this was not unreasonable. Zang Hong should originally have not gone to Yuan Shao for troops, nor should he later have become an enemy of those who had been his allies. For Zang Hong's sake, if his strength was insufficient, he could have fled to another state in order to seek help. If his plans and forces had not yet matured and he wished to wait for the right opportunity, then it would have been better to remain patient, watch for a chance, and die in service of Cheng Chao. Why must one swear to defend an isolated city without flexibility, leading to his own death and the destruction of his people, achieving neither merit nor reputation—how truly pitiable! Chen Rong, a native of Zang Hong's commandery, was originally a scholar who personally admired Zang Hong and followed him as assistant Tai Shou of Dongjun. Before the city fell, Zang Hong sent him out. When Cao Shao had him seated among the guests, Chen Rong saw that Zang Hong was about to be executed and rose to say: "General, you have undertaken great affairs with the aim of ridding the world of tyrants. Yet now you kill a loyal and righteous man first—how can this conform to Heaven's will! Zang Hong was appointed Tai Shou by the commandery; how then could you kill him!" Cao Shao felt ashamed and had his attendants drag Chen Rong out, saying: "You are no equal of Zang Hong; you have only wasted your words!" Chen Rong looked back and said: "Is righteousness not a constant? Those who follow it are gentlemen, those who betray it are small-minded men. Today I would rather die on the same day as Zang Hong than live another day with you, General!" He was killed again. Earlier, Zang Hong had sent out two military secretaries to seek aid from Lu Bu; By the time they returned, the city had already fallen. Both rushed into battle and died.
